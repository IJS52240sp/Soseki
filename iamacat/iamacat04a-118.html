煙草たばこの火ひはだんだん吸口すいくちの方ほうへ逼せまって、一寸いっすんばかり燃もえ尽つくした灰はいの棒ぼうがぱたりと毛布の上に落おつるのも構かまわず主人は一生懸命いっしょうけんめいに煙草から立たち上のぼる煙けむりの行末ゆくすえを見詰みつめている。その煙けむりは春風しゅんぷうに浮うきつ沈しずみつ、流ながれる輪わを幾重いくえにも描えがいて、紫深むらさきふかき細君さいくんの洗髪あらいがみの根本ねもとへ吹ふき寄よせつつある。――おや、細君の事を話はなしておくはずだった。忘わすれていた。

Rough translation

The master has spread a white blanket there on the veranda, plopped himself down onto his belly, and is basking in the bright spring sun. The sun's rays are remarkably impartial, imparting the same radiant warmth to this humble abode with weeds sprouting from its roof as they do the the Kaneda's fine drawing room. The blanket, though, is woefully ill-matched to the day at hand. It was woven as white, shipped and sold as white, and the master even went and bought it for its fair white shade. -- However, that was twelve or thirteen years prior, and its time of whiteness is long past. It's now well into its discoloration phase, ashen-gray in hue. Whether there's enough life in it to go darker yet is an open question. It's thoroughly frayed and threadbare, and to call it a blanket anymore is downright presumptuous. "Blank" is the better term. In the master's mind, though, if it's lasted a year, or two, or five, or even ten, then it's good for a lifetime. He plays it day by day. As to what he's doing, lying on his belly on his cherished blanket, his chin is propped on both hands, and a cigarette is wedged between the fingers of his right hand. That' it. Of course, there may be profound cosmic truths racing through his dandruff-covered head like chariots of fire, but I have to say this seems most unlikely.

His cigarette burns slowly toward his mouth. His attention is fixed on the smoke that rises from its tip. He follows the whisps with his gaze, oblivious to the column of ash that crumbles away and drops onto his blanket. The smoke floats and sinks on the spring breeze, drifting round in myriad layers and grazing the dark roots of the wife's freshly washed hair. -- Ah yes, did I neglect to mention the wife?
